Attractions

  • Top of the Rock

    One of the best views of NYC, doing either this or the One World Observatory is a must (the Empire State Building is good too). You can go all throughout the day, but I would recommend trying to get tickets for a time 30 minutes before sunset. That way, you can see the city light up once the sun goes down!

    •

  • Central Park

    Another one of New York City's most recognizable landmarks, Central Park is absolutely MASSIVE. There's so much to explore, inclduing a little lake that you can take paddleboats out on and a zoo. Go for a run, have a picnic, or watch street performers - whatever suits your fancy!

    •

  • The High Line

    The High Line is another urban park, but with a twist: this elevated pedestrian walkway takes you right beside big buildings and a bustling city! There are vendors along the way and plenty of great lookout points. Very pleasant if you want to walk without having to worry about streetlights! Make sure to visit Chelsea Market when you reach the end!

    •

  • Radio City Music Hall

    Widely known for the Rockettes, this is a wonderful, historic music hall. They have all sorts of concerts here, but I would highly recommend the Christmas Spectacular starrting the Radio City Rockettes if you're in New York during the holidays!

  • Times Square

    As long as you don't mind crowds, Times Square is pretty cool! Another quintessential New York landmark, you can sit here for hours just watching people and advertisements. Come on New Year's Eve for the ball drop if you're brave enough to brave the elements and the crowds.

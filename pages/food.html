Food

  • Chelsea Market

    You could find almost any kind of food that you want here, from Mexican to Mediterranean to Italian ice cream! It's a little bazaar-like area with plenty of restaurants to choose from.

    ("Chelsea Market" by s o d a p o p is licensed under CC BY-ND 2.0)

  • Juliana's/Grimaldi's Pizza

    You've probably heard of New York's legendary pizza, but have you tasted it? If not (or even if you have), this should be your first stop! These two pizza parlors are right next each other. The story goes that Grimaldi's was very famous, but then changed its dough recipe. The main chef wasn't too hapy about it, so he branched off and formed Juliana's which sticks to the original recipe. Which is better? You decide! Just beware, there might be some pretty long lines!

    ("Juliana's Pizza" by Shinya Suzuki is licensed under CC BY-ND 2.0)

  • Smorgasburg

    Smorgasburg is, as its name suggests, a smorgasbord of food trucks, tents, and small vendors offering all sorts of meals and treats. During the summer they hang out at Prospect Park, while they move indoors at Atlantic Center during the winter. Usually they set up shop right next to Brooklyn Flea, considered by many to be a "must-see" in Brooklyn.

    ("Chilling...." by Jana Markova is licensed under CC BY 2.0)

  • F. Ottomanelli Burgers

    This joint in Queens is touted as "the best hamburger in New York City", and I can attest that this place really does live up to the hype! It originated when one of the Ottomanellis, a famous family of butchers in New York, had the idea to make a quality hamburger. They use the finest ingredients to craft their menu, including Häagen-Dazs ice cream in their milk shakes!

  • Levain

    A classic but really tasty bakery, Levain's offerings include some enormous, scrumptious cookies! You can find them at various locations in the city. Delectable!
